## Why

Every demo version so far has been hand-crafted: the system-map was a fixture,
the nav-bundle came from a separate scan with incompatible IDs (`repo:` vs
`component:`), and the semantic investigation was a curated fixture. The
result was broken links, dead surfaces, and a product that looks like a
placeholder.

The published demo SHALL be produced by running the full Portolan pipeline
end-to-end on a real target — scan → system-map → nav-bundle → SI → export —
with coherent IDs across all stages and zero hand-crafted data in the live
path.

## What Changes

- The demo atlas SHALL be generated by the full pipeline, not from a
  hand-crafted system-map fixture.
- All artifact IDs SHALL be coherent across stages (no `repo:` vs
  `component:` mismatches).
- The fixture-backed path is retained for tests, not for the published demo.
- The demo target SHALL be a real JVM landscape (Apache Bigtop) where the
  multi-language manifest detection and source reference detection produce
  real edges.

## Capabilities

### Modified Capabilities

- `reading-experience`: the demo provenance is from a real scan.
- `engineering-standards`: the pipeline SHALL produce coherent IDs across
  stages.

## Impact

- Depends on: `multi-language-dependency-detection` (manifest edges) +
  `jvm-source-references` (structural edges). Without these, the demo map
  is disconnected nodes.
- Out of scope: the visual design of the demo (separate concern).

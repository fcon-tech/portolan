# Semantic Component Investigation Specification

## Purpose

Defines how a selected component investigation explains the component's place in
the ecosystem, its purpose, its internal model, its integrations, its risks and
peculiarities, and its overlaps or alternatives — as a portable capability
proven on a small swappable sample, with every semantic assertion labeled by
source boundary. This is a semantic system landscape, not a repository catalog.

## Requirements

### Requirement: Investigations teach semantic meaning, not repository metadata
A component investigation SHALL explain where the component sits in the
ecosystem, why it exists, how it is internally structured at a useful
architectural level, how it integrates with neighboring capabilities, what
overlaps or competes with it, and what is risky, unusual, or operationally
important. An investigation that could be produced by listing repositories,
labels, and external links MUST NOT be accepted.

#### Scenario: An investigation teaches more than a repo list
- GIVEN a selected component investigation is opened
- WHEN the admiral reads it
- THEN it explains ecosystem placement, purpose, internal model, integration, risks, and overlaps
- AND its content could not be generated by listing repositories and labels alone

### Requirement: Capability is the organizing unit, not the repository
The semantic layer SHALL organize the landscape around capabilities rather than
repositories. A component MAY provide multiple capabilities, and multiple
components MAY overlap on one capability. The investigation MUST NOT assume a
component is equivalent to a repository.

#### Scenario: Components share a capability
- GIVEN two distinct components provide the same capability
- WHEN the admiral inspects the capability region
- THEN both components appear against that capability
- AND the mapping is not one-component-to-one-repository

### Requirement: Source boundary labels every semantic assertion
Every semantic assertion SHALL carry a source boundary of `local-corpus`,
`curated-knowledge`, `agent-hypothesis`, or `not_assessed`. Curated-knowledge
claims MUST carry a resolvable source card (official docs, project docs, a
checked-in curated note, or another resolvable artifact); invisible
"the model knows this" sourcing MUST NOT be permitted. The boundary MUST be
readable without forcing the admiral to open raw evidence first.

#### Scenario: A curated claim carries a resolvable source card
- GIVEN an investigation contains a curated-knowledge claim
- WHEN the admiral reads it
- THEN the claim exposes its source boundary and a resolvable source card
- AND the boundary is visible before opening raw evidence

### Requirement: Internal model requires concepts or an explicit not-assessed boundary
Each investigated component SHALL present at least five internal concepts, each
with a plain-language explanation and source-boundary label, unless the page
explicitly states the internal model is `not_assessed` and names the concrete
next producer needed to assess it. A selected sample MUST NOT pass if every
component uses the `not_assessed` escape hatch.

#### Scenario: Internal concepts are explained or honestly deferred
- GIVEN an investigated component is opened
- WHEN its internal model renders
- THEN at least five concepts are explained with source boundaries
- OR the page explicitly states not_assessed and names the next producer needed

### Requirement: Risks and peculiarities are component-specific
Each investigated component SHALL show at least two component-specific risks or
peculiarities. Generic text such as "inspect source" or "runtime not assessed"
MUST NOT count toward the minimum.

#### Scenario: Generic boilerplate does not satisfy the risk minimum
- GIVEN an investigated component is opened
- WHEN its risks render
- THEN at least two component-specific risks or peculiarities appear
- AND generic "inspect source" or "runtime not assessed" text is not counted

### Requirement: Overlap and alternatives are bidirectional
The selected sample SHALL include at least one bidirectional overlap or
alternative relation between components. Opening either side of the pair MUST
show the other side and explain the overlap dimensions. `overlaps_with` MUST be
treated as a semantic relation, not a dependency edge.

#### Scenario: An overlap pair reads symmetrically
- GIVEN the sample contains a selected overlap pair
- WHEN the admiral opens either component in the pair
- THEN the other component is shown as an overlapping or alternative component
- AND the relation is bidirectional and explains the overlap dimensions

### Requirement: Ecosystem placement map shows capability regions
The atlas SHALL include at least one semantic ecosystem map or panel that shows
capability regions and places the selected components into them, shows at least
one visible overlap or alternative relation, links from a placed component to
its investigation page, and makes source boundaries or not-assessed areas
visible enough to avoid false completeness. The map MUST NOT be the existing
repository graph relabeled.

#### Scenario: The placement map is organized by capability
- GIVEN the atlas is generated
- WHEN the admiral opens the ecosystem placement map
- THEN capability regions and placed components are visible
- AND at least one overlap or alternative relation is shown
- AND the map is not a relabeled repository graph

### Requirement: Meaning is separable from evidence
A component investigation SHALL be readable as a main story without first
opening raw evidence, while each claim group exposes whether it is
`local-corpus`, `curated-knowledge`, `agent-hypothesis`, or `not_assessed`.
The page MUST NOT collapse these into a single confidence badge.

#### Scenario: The story reads before the evidence
- GIVEN a component page contains curated knowledge and local corpus evidence
- WHEN the admiral reads the page
- THEN the main story is readable without opening raw evidence
- AND each claim group exposes its source boundary rather than a single collapsed badge

### Requirement: Sample is swappable and not hardcoded to named technologies
The investigation contract SHALL be proven on a small selected sample that
includes at least one component with a clear ecosystem capability, at least one
pair with a meaningful semantic overlap or alternative, and at least one
component whose local corpus evidence is incomplete forcing an honest
source-boundary display. Acceptance MUST NOT be written around fixed named
technologies; the reviewer MUST be able to swap the sample for another eligible
set without rewriting the contract.

#### Scenario: A different eligible sample still satisfies the contract
- GIVEN the sample is swapped for another eligible set of components
- WHEN the investigation contract is evaluated
- THEN the same requirements apply without contract changes

### Requirement: Broken or content-poor pages fail acceptance
A component investigation MUST NOT render `[object Object]`, `undefined`,
`Object not found`, or generic repository-only copy as its primary explanation,
and no first-class visible link on a selected component page MAY fall back to a
generic component or repository page for any selected component.

#### Scenario: Broken rendered values are treated as failures
- GIVEN the generated atlas is opened
- WHEN every first-class visible link on selected component pages is activated
- THEN no destination shows [object Object], undefined, or Object not found
- AND no destination falls back to a generic component or repository page

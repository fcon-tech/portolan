## Overall Verdict

**Spec 060 fixes the read-only posture and prevents Bigtop service startup correctly, but introduces an evidence-state honesty regression in its own classification.** The probes ran successfully, found no Bigtop observations, and produced documented negative results. That's `cannot_verify`, not `not_assessed`. Calling successful probes "not assessed" when they delivered concrete negative evidence undermines Constitution II and FR-005. The Cursor stress assessment remains `claim-only` because the actual model outputs aren't available. The dual-state explanation GLM accepted doesn't fix this — it explains why two different things are different, not why the probes' own results are mislabeled.

The spec prevents overclaiming in its Claim Boundary (which is well-written) but overclaims subtly in its own ledger body language ("verifies the absence") and in its classification labels (`not_assessed` for probes that ran and produced results). These are fixable with terminology changes and committing the stress outputs.
